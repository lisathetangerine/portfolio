Course structure

The course is structured in three phases.
Each student is appointed a personal tutor to support them during their studies.

Semester 1

The course will begin by covering the foundation skills in both journalism and coding. On the coding side this will mean learning the basics of computing languages like Python and Web Development, while on the journalism side it will see students taught the essentials, including media law. Students will also attend a cross computing/journalism set of workshops and seminars, which support harmonisation of the skills being developed through each of the subject areas. The course timetable can be downloaded here.

All modules listed below are core in this phase:

  • Digital Journalism
  • Web Application Development
  • Information Processing in Python
  • Reporters and the Reported

Semester 2

As the year progresses, the students will use the skills acquired over the first semester to do investigative journalism. The School of Journalism, in fact, will offer a new compulsory module, Digital Investigation, as an introduction to the history and current issues in computer-assisted reporting.

Furthermore, students will choose a computing module from:

  • Web and Social Computing
  • Human Centric Computing
  • Visual Communication and Information Design

And two journalism modules from:

  • Reporting Business, Finance and Economics
  • Reporting Health and Science
  • Global Crisis Reporting
  • Managing Print in a Digital World

Dissertation project

The project component is the last and third stage of the programme. At this point, students will carry out individual research and development of a Dissertation project using the editorial and computational skills they have learned in the first two phases of the course.

Students who wish to exit the programme early may be awarded a PgDip or PgCert.
