The City

Cardiff is the capital and the largest city in Wales, it has more than adequate shops, nightlife and the Millennium Stadium. Though, it is compact and manageable, getting around is quick and easy, you can get all of the facilities expected of any modern capital but without any stress, everything is within walking distance. It is also well connected to the rest of the country by train.

Additionally, it is one of the most competitive and, at the same time, affordable cities in the UK. Perfect for students, the Welsh capital has more green space per person than any core UK city. Wherever you are, a park or garden is never far away!

Cardiff University

Founded in 1883, the University of Cardiff is a member of the Russell Group Universities. We have over 300 courses that you can tailor to your ambitions and interests and we maintain the highest standards of teaching to ensure students gain knowledge, skills and experience which are of lifelong value.

Besides, we are one of the few universities in Britain to offer a dedicated Graduate Centre for postgraduate students, which provides year-round facilities and services designed to enhance your experience including meeting and study facilities, and a programme of events.

Students' Union

Are you new in the city? Do you want to expand your friendship networks?

Cardiff University Students' Union (CUSU) is a charity offering services, resources and support, which use money made through their bars, cafes, shops and events to improve the services offered to students. Our aim is to enhance your university experience and make sure that your time in Cardiff is unforgettable!
